Understanding Life's Essential 8 (8th Grade Level)

Heart disease and stroke are some of the top causes of illness and death for American Indian people. To help people live longer and healthier lives, the American Heart Association (AHA) created a set of 8 goals in 2022 called Life's Essential 8. These goals include:

  • Eating a healthy diet
  • Getting regular physical activity
  • Avoiding nicotine (like smoking or vaping)
  • Getting good sleep
  • Keeping a healthy body weight
  • Managing cholesterol
  • Managing blood pressure
  • Managing blood sugar

In this study, researchers looked at over 2,100 American Indian adults who were part of the Strong Heart Family Study. These participants didn't have heart disease when the study began. Researchers gave each person a heart health score from 0 to 100, based on how well they followed 7 of the 8 AHA goals (sleep wasn't included because the study didn't collect sleep data).

What Did They Find?

  • People with moderate heart health were 48% less likely to develop heart disease than those with low heart health.
  • People with high heart health were 75% less likely to develop heart disease.

Why This Matters

This study shows that following the AHA's Life's Essential 8 habits can make a big difference in preventing heart disease. It highlights the need for programs and resources that support heart-healthy living in American Indian communities.

Key Takeaways

Over 2,100 American Indian adults from the Strong Heart Family Study were followed to examine the relationship between Life's Essential 8 health behaviors and cardiovascular disease. Participants with moderate heart health scores (50-69) had 48% lower risk, while those with high scores (70-100) had 75% lower risk of developing heart disease compared to those with low scores.

Did You Know? The Life's Essential 8 framework, updated by the American Heart Association in 2022, added sleep health to the original Life's Simple 7 metrics. This study demonstrates these evidence-based health behaviors significantly reduce cardiovascular disease risk in American Indian populations.
