Diabetes & Health

Understanding Diabetes in Our Community

Diabetes affects about 19% of our community members. That's nearly 1 in 5 people. Understanding the connection between diabetes and other health factors can help us make better choices.

Weight and Diabetes

The chart shows how diabetes rates increase with higher body weight:

  • Normal weight: 5.7% have diabetes
  • Overweight: 13.7% have diabetes
  • Obese: 24.4% have diabetes
  • Severely obese: 28.6% have diabetes

Maintaining a healthy weight is one way to lower diabetes risk.

Traditional Foods and Health

Traditional foods like beans, corn, squash, berries, and wild plants have nourished our ancestors for generations. Many of these foods are rich in nutrients that help prevent chronic diseases. Combining traditional wisdom with modern nutrition knowledge can help us eat in ways that support our health.

Physical Activity

Staying Active for Health

Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy weight, reduces the risk of chronic diseases, and improves overall well-being. The data shows how activity levels in our community connect to health outcomes.

Current Activity Levels

The average community member takes about 4,944 steps per day. Health experts often recommend a goal of 10,000 steps daily, though even smaller increases can provide health benefits.

Current Average 4,944 steps (49% of goal)

Even adding 2,000 more steps per day (about 20 minutes of walking) could significantly improve health outcomes in our community.

Traditional Activities

Traditional activities like dancing, gathering, hunting, and gardening have kept our communities active for generations. These activities not only provide physical benefits but also connect us to our culture and community.

Health Recommendations

Small Steps Toward Better Health

Based on the data, here are some recommendations that could help improve health in our community. Remember that even small changes can make a big difference over time.

Nutrition Recommendations

  • Aim for at least 3-5 servings of vegetables each day (current average: 2.6)
  • Increase fruit intake to at least 2 servings daily (current average: 1.0)
  • Choose whole grains like wild rice, corn, or whole wheat over refined grains
  • Consider traditional foods like beans, berries, and wild plants as part of a balanced diet
  • Limit processed foods, sugary drinks, and high-fat meats

Physical Activity Recommendations

  • Aim to increase daily steps gradually (current average: 4,944 steps)
  • Try to add 2,000 steps (about 20 minutes of walking) to your daily routine
  • Incorporate traditional activities like dancing or gardening
  • Make activity a family or community event to help everyone stay motivated
  • Start small - even 10 minutes of movement several times a day can help

Diabetes Prevention

  • Maintain a healthy weight through balanced nutrition and regular activity
  • Get regular health screenings to catch early warning signs
  • Know your family history - if diabetes runs in your family, prevention is especially important
  • If you have diabetes, work with healthcare providers to manage it effectively
  • Join community wellness programs for support and motivation

About This Data

This dashboard presents information from a health assessment of 2,779 community members. The data comes from the "phase4diet_preliminary_250331.csv" dataset, which contains information about various health indicators, including:

  • Demographics (age, gender)
  • Health measures (BMI, cholesterol, diabetes status)
  • Lifestyle factors (smoking, physical activity)
  • Nutrition patterns (servings of different food groups)

The goal of this dashboard is to help community members understand health patterns and make informed choices. The information is presented in a way that respects privacy - no individual's data can be identified.

Understanding these patterns can help us work together to improve health outcomes in our community. By combining traditional wisdom with current health knowledge, we can develop approaches that honor our heritage while addressing today's challenges.

This dashboard was created to support community health initiatives. The data is anonymous and is presented to help inform, not to judge or label individuals.
