Pre-pandemic Lung Function and COVID-19 Risk

Study from the C4R Cohort

üìö Summary

This study looked at how well people could breathe before the COVID-19 pandemic and how that affected their chances of getting very sick from the virus. Doctors used a test called spirometry to measure how well people's lungs worked. They found that people with more serious lung problems, like trouble blowing air out or breathing in deeply, were more likely to be hospitalized or die from COVID-19. This was true even for people who didn't have other big health problems. The researchers say we should help protect people with poor lung function more during things like pandemics.
