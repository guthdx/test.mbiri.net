Heart disease is the number one cause of death for American Indian women. This study looked at whether having more children or problems during pregnancy (like high blood pressure or diabetes) made women more likely to get heart disease later in life.

Researchers followed over 1,200 American Indian women for about 13‚Äì16 years. They found that women who had five or more children or who had problems during pregnancy were a little more likely to get heart disease‚Äîbut the results weren't strong enough to prove for sure.

Even though it wasn't a clear "yes," the study shows these could be important warning signs. Doctors might want to watch women with many children or past pregnancy problems more closely for heart disease.

Key Takeaways

This 13-16 year study of over 1,200 American Indian women from the Strong Heart Family Study examined the link between reproductive history and cardiovascular disease. While the association between having five or more children or pregnancy complications and heart disease risk wasn't statistically definitive, the findings suggest these factors may serve as important warning signs for future cardiovascular health.
