Main Findings

Using epigenome-wide association study (EWAS) data, researchers identified multiple DNA methylation sites that were associated with both cancer and cardiovascular outcomes. These included sites related to inflammation, immune function, and cell aging. The findings suggest shared pathways between major chronic diseases and open doors to predictive biomarker development.
